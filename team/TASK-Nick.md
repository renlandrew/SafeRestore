# Task Card — Nick · Teach / Learning

**Mission:** Turn the safety method into something that **teaches people to catch AI errors** — and prove
with data that it actually works.

> Read `README.md` (ground rules) first. You own the **Teach** surface + the learning study.

## Your research question & hypothesis
- **Question:** Do people who practice with clean/degraded/restored CT get better at spotting AI hallucinations
  than people who just read about it?
- **Hypothesis:** Hands-on practice beats reading — measurably higher error-detection accuracy after.

## What to do (each step has a "done =")
**Step 1 — Lock the learner (Week 0–1)**
- One sentence: *who* you teach + *what* they'll be able to do (suggest: high schoolers → spot AI-altered CT).
- List 3 concrete skills (e.g. "tell a real edge from a hallucinated one").
- **done =** learning objective + 3 skill points written down.

**Step 2 — Build the micro-lesson (Week 1–2)**
- Expand the Teach surface into **3–4 short steps**; use **David's real failure cases** as the quiz items.
- **done =** a lesson someone else can finish start-to-end without you explaining.

**Step 3 — Your experiment: pre/post study (Week 2) ← this is your core science**
- Recruit **≥10 peers**. Pre-test (score them spotting AI-altered images) → lesson → post-test (new images).
- If you can, split into **tool group vs text-only group** (a control) to make it stronger.
- **done =** an anonymous score table (pre vs post, and tool vs control if you managed it).

**Step 4 — Analyze + memo (Week 3)**
- Make one figure: pre vs post (and tool vs control). State the limits honestly (small n, informal).
- **done =** 1-page Learning-Impact memo: what it shows · what it does NOT prove · the boundary.

**Step 5 — Capstone + demo (Week 3–4)**
- Final lesson step = the learner runs Use + Prove themselves and submits an exported Evidence Pack.
- Record a 30–45s Teach demo clip for the video.
- **done =** capstone flow + demo clip.

## Resources you'll mainly use
`saferestore-mvp/index.html` (Teach surface) · David's failure cases · Worksheet Parts 2, 9, 10 · the
education sources in `RESOURCES.md`.

## Your guardrails (on top of the team rules)
- **Do NOT build a whole learning platform.** One lesson + one study. Depth over breadth.
- **Study ethics:** voluntary, verbal-consent, **anonymous scores only** (no names, no personal data).
- **Honest stats:** with n≈10 you can't claim significance — say "early signal," show the numbers, note limits.
- **Don't fake engagement:** if the lesson doesn't help, report that — it's still a finding.

## What you deliver to the pitch
- The **impact proof** (your differentiator vs every product-only team): "our tool measurably improved
  people's ability to catch unsafe AI — here's the before/after."
- The **market + business model** for the education side: med schools / bio classrooms, and how it's sold.

## Judge questions to prep
- "How big is the education market / who pays?" → med schools, bio/health classes; tie to a source.
- "How do you know your tool actually teaches?" → your pre/post data (with honest limits).
- "Isn't this just a quiz app?" → no — it teaches the one idea behind safe medical AI: sharper ≠ correct.
